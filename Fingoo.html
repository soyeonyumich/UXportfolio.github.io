The Solutions
1. Investment Info
Users can easily determine whether a stock has increased or decreased through the use of graph visuals and color-coded text.
Users can also share stock information with others.
2. Search Charts
Users can easily search for investment information to share.
This feature is designed for individuals who want to easily share investment information with others on the community page.
3.Calender
Users can view investment information filtered by time, country, and importance. Time options include yesterday, today, tomorrow, this week, and next week.
In addition, users can choose from calendar options such as economic, earnings, dividend, and IPO calendars. Users can access more detailed information by clicking on the investment data.
4.Community
Users can share their opinions and investment information on the community page by posting text, images, and stock data.
It will recommend posts by the newest updates, or users can choose to view only posts from accounts they follow.

The Ideation

Persona
Our main client's target users are investors in their 20s and 30s. After conducting 30 user interviews, our team developed two personas to represent their needs.
Our primary focus is on supporting individual and professional investors, as they have a greater need for well-organized, easily understandable, yet also trustworthy and approachable investment information.
User Flow
After brainstorming, I created a user flow to organize the structure of the mobile app that will guide users in navigating to reach their 3 end goals:
  1. Home page
  2. Community
  3. Setting

The Design & Iteration

Website Design
I designed the app to include features similar to the website. After several meetings, the client asked to add a community feature and allow users to view specific investment information, which the website didn't have. I created a unified design system for both the web and app to ensure a consistent look and experience across both platforms
Wire-frame
The sketch feedback
Based on user feedback and testing with 6 selected users from our respondents, several areas for improvement have been identified in the design of our mobile app prototype
  1. Icon color:
    Users noted that the like icon color didn't look aesthetic and didn't match the color palette, suggesting it be made lighter for better harmony.
  2. Main page community
    Users expressed a preference to see the most popular community posts featured on the main page.
  3. Community
    The app previously had a different navigation bar for the community section, but users preferred having the same navigation bar across the app for consistency.
  4. Chart Search:
    Users requested a feature that allows them to easily search for charts and share them on the community page
